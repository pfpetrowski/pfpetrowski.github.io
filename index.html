A bit about me

I am a data research scientist at Benson Hill . I like to solve problems by building things. Most of my time is spent developing software to address problems that face biologists, especially in dealing with large genomic datasets. I am committed to building solutions that are adaptable and scalable to any configuration of the problem space.

Formerly I was a graduate student in the King Lab at the University of Missouri. There I worked at the intersection of computational biology and population genetics to build tools for genomic inference. Before that I studied Molecular Biology at Stetson University where I did research in molecular genetics.

My interests include machine learning, predictive analytics, and high throughput computing.

I'm also really fun at parties.
